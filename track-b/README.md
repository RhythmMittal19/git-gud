# Track B: Deep Fundamentals

> [Back to main README](../README.md)

**Status: Deferred until employed.** Get the job first, then go deep.

### Track B Lite (Optional: During Employment)

If you want to start learning deep concepts while employed, here's a minimal approach:

**Weekend Schedule (2-3 hours Saturday OR Sunday):**

```text
Month 1-3:   Read "CODE" by Charles Petzold (1 chapter/week)
Month 4-6:   Nand2Tetris Part 1 (1 project every 2 weeks)
Month 7-9:   C basics (K&R or Beej's Guide)
Month 10+:   Choose based on interest/career needs
```

**Books to Read (One at a Time):**

1. **CODE** by Charles Petzold — How computers work (beginner-friendly)
2. **The C Programming Language** by K&R — Learn C properly
3. **Operating Systems: Three Easy Pieces** — Free, readable OS book
4. **Designing Data-Intensive Applications** — Modern systems bible

---

### When Track B Becomes Critical

Track B moves from "nice to have" to "must have" when:

1. **Targeting senior roles** (5+ years experience)
2. **Working on infrastructure/systems** (databases, compilers, OS)
3. **Performance-critical applications** (gaming, trading systems)
4. **Interviewing at top companies** (Google, Meta, etc. ask systems design)
5. **Building developer tools** (compilers, frameworks, libraries)
6. **Curiosity** — You genuinely want to understand how things work

For your first 1-2 years: **Track A skills are sufficient.** Focus on shipping products, gaining experience, and learning on the job.
