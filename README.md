---

## The Three Laws

**1. Foundation Before Framework** — Learn JavaScript deeply before React. Learn to walk before you run.

**2. Build, Don't Just Watch** — For every hour of learning, spend two hours building. Tutorials feel productive but aren't.

**3. Understand, Don't Memorize** — If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it yet.

**4. Done > Perfect** — A deployed messy project beats a perfect one on your laptop. Perfectionism is procrastination in disguise.

---

## AI Policy

I use AI to **learn** (explain concepts, review my code, suggest what to study next).

I do NOT use AI to **write code**. Not for boilerplate, not for DSA, not for projects.

Why? Because I'm building toward a startup. If I can't code without AI, I can't debug without AI, I can't architect without AI, and I can't hire engineers I can evaluate. The shortcut today is the bottleneck tomorrow.

If you're just starting and don't know where to begin:

2. Then [Track A: HTML](track-a/01-html/README.md) — build your first web page
3. Keep going. Don't skip ahead. Trust the process.

If you're a complete beginner, read [docs/philosophy.md](docs/philosophy.md) first. It'll save you months of wasted effort.
