# Schedule for Working Developers

> [Back to main README](../README.md) | [Original full-time schedule](schedule.md)

The original roadmap assumes 65 hrs/week of full-time learning. If you're working a job (like 11am-7pm), here's the adjusted version.

---

## The Reality

You have a job. Congratulations — that's the whole point of Track A.

But learning doesn't stop. You just shift from "sprint mode" to "marathon mode." The timeline stretches from 6 months to 10-12 months, but the destination is the same.

**The math:**
- Full-time learner: ~65 hrs/week = 6 months
- Working developer: ~25 hrs/week = 10-12 months
- The content doesn't change. Only the pace.

---

## Weekday Schedule (Working Day)

```text
07:00 - 08:30   Morning block: DSA OR Foundation (1.5 hrs)
                 → Fresh brain = best for hard problems
08:30 - 09:00   Break + get ready for work
09:00 - 10:30   Free buffer (commute, prep, or extra study)

11:00 - 19:00   JOB — this is sacred, don't mix learning here
                 → But DO observe: what patterns does production code use?
                 → What tools does the team rely on? That's free learning.

19:30 - 20:00   Decompress — walk, eat, NO SCREENS
20:00 - 22:00   Evening block: Track A (2 hrs)
                 → Build mode: projects, exercises, real code
22:00 - 22:30   Review what you learned + plan tomorrow
22:30 - 06:30   Sleep (8 hrs) — NON-NEGOTIABLE
```

**Weekday total: 3-3.5 hrs of focused coding**

---

## Sunday Schedule (Free Day)

```text
08:00 - 12:00   Deep work: Track A project (4 hrs)
                 → This is where real projects happen
12:00 - 13:00   Lunch
13:00 - 16:00   DSA practice OR project work (3 hrs)
16:00 onwards   FULL REST — no code, no guilt
```

**Sunday total: 7 hrs**

## What Changes, What Doesn't

**Changes:**
- Timeline: 6 months → 10-12 months
- Daily hours: 10-11 → 3-3.5
- DSA: daily → 3-4 sessions/week
- Job search: N/A (you already have a job)

**Doesn't change:**
- The content (same material, same depth)
- The philosophy (understand > memorize)
- The quality bar (no shortcuts)
- Sleep, exercise, rest (still non-negotiable)

---

## Bonus: Learning From Your Job

Your day job is free education. Pay attention to:

- How the codebase is structured (copy the good patterns)
- What tools the team uses (learn them deeply)
- How PRs are reviewed (absorb the feedback)
- How senior devs debug (watch their process)
- What breaks in production (those are the best lessons)

Write down one thing you learned at work each day. In 6 months, that's 180 real-world lessons no course can teach you.

---

## When Motivation Dies

It will. Around month 3-4 of working + learning, you'll want to quit the learning part.

When that happens:
1. Do 15 minutes. Just 15. That's it.
2. If after 15 minutes you still hate it, stop. No guilt.
3. But most days, 15 minutes turns into an hour.
4. Consistency beats intensity. 15 min/day > 5 hrs once a week.

The people who make it aren't the most talented. They're the ones who didn't stop.
