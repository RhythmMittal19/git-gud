# Philosophy & Principles

> Extracted from the main README. This is the heart of git-gud.

### The Goal

You will not just "learn to code." You will understand computers so deeply that:

1. Any new language takes days, not months
2. Any new framework is just syntax over concepts you already know
3. Bugs that stump others are obvious to you
4. You see the matrix — bytes, memory, network packets, everything

**But first:** You need a job. The goal for the first 6 months is employment. Deep understanding comes after financial stability.

### The Three Laws

**Law 1: Foundation Before Framework**
Learn JavaScript deeply before React. Learn C before Go. Learn TCP before HTTP.
_(Analogy: Learn to walk before you run, but don't wait until you can run a marathon before leaving the house)_

**Law 2: Build, Don't Just Watch**
For every hour of learning, spend two hours building. No exceptions.
Tutorials feel productive but aren't. Building is where learning happens.

**Law 3: Understand, Don't Memorize**
If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it.
_(Don't cry — take your time and read again. Understanding takes repetition.)_

### The Fourth Law (New)

**Law 4: Done > Perfect**

- A deployed messy project > A perfect project on your laptop
- A sent application with a typo > A resume you're still editing
- Code that works > Code you're still "improving"

Perfectionism is procrastination in disguise.

### Why Multiple Tracks?

| Track      | Purpose                              | Time Investment | Payoff Timeline |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------ | --------------- | --------------- |
| Foundation | Skills that multiply everything else | 1-2 hrs/day     | Immediate       |
| Track A    | Employable skills, income            | 5-7 hrs/day     | 5-6 months      |
| DSA        | Pass interview gates                 | 1-2 hrs/day     | 4-6 months      |
| Job Search | Actually getting interviews          | 1-2 hrs/day     | Month 4+        |
| Track B    | Deep understanding (deferred)        | Weekends only   | 2-3 years       |

**They reinforce each other:**

- Foundation makes both tracks faster
- Track A pays bills while you master Track B
- DSA gets you past interview gates
- Track B makes you exceptional at Track A (long-term)

## Final Notes

### The Reality Check

**This roadmap is aggressive.** Here's how to sustain it:

```text
1. SLEEP IS NON-NEGOTIABLE
   - 7+ hours minimum
   - Your brain consolidates learning during sleep
   - Cutting sleep to study more is counterproductive

2. EXERCISE IS NON-NEGOTIABLE
   - Badminton, walking, anything
   - Exercise improves cognitive function
   - Your brain works better when your body moves

3. REST IS PRODUCTIVE
   - Take one full evening off per week
   - Take one full day off per month
   - Burnout sets you back weeks

4. PROGRESS > PERFECTION
   - Done is better than perfect
   - You don't need to understand 100% before moving on
   - You'll revisit topics anyway

5. ADJUST AS NEEDED
   - If something takes longer, that's okay
   - Life happens
   - The timeline is a guide, not a law
```

### The Path Forward

**What to expect:**

```text
MONTH 1-2: Overwhelm
- There's so much you don't know
- Every concept reveals ten more
- This is normal. Push through.

MONTH 3-4: Clicking
- Concepts start connecting
- Code you wrote before looks embarrassingly bad
- That's growth!

MONTH 5-6: Building
- You can build real things
- Interviews feel less scary
- You're starting to see yourself as a developer

MONTH 7-10: Employment
- Getting interviews
- Learning from rejections
- Eventually, an offer

YEAR 2+: Growth
- Learning on the job
- Track B on weekends
- Becoming genuinely good

YEAR 3+: Mastery
- Deep understanding
- Can learn anything quickly
- The irreplaceable developer
```

### When You Feel Lost

**It will happen. Here's what to do:**

```text
1. GO BACK TO BASICS
   - Reread, redo, rebuild
   - Sometimes you moved too fast
   - It's okay to go back

2. TEACH WHAT YOU KNOW
   - Explain a concept to someone
   - Write a blog post
   - Teaching reveals gaps

3. BUILD SOMETHING
   - Theory without practice is useless
   - If stuck in tutorial hell, just build
   - Messy code > no code

4. ASK FOR HELP
   - Stack Overflow
   - Discord communities
   - Reddit (r/learnprogramming)
   - No question is too basic

5. TAKE A BREAK
   - Sometimes the brain needs rest
   - Go for a walk
   - Sleep on it
   - Answers come when you stop trying
```

### The Mindset

```text
"I am not talented. I am obsessed."
    - Become obsessed with understanding

"Done is better than perfect."
    - Ship it. You can improve later.

"The best time to plant a tree was 20 years ago.
 The second best time is now."
    - Start today. Not Monday. Today.

"Consistency beats intensity."
    - 1 hour daily > 10 hours once a week

"Imposter syndrome means you're growing."
    - If it's easy, you're not learning
```
