## Job Search Strategy

> **When to Start:** Month 4 (don't wait until you're "ready")
> **Daily Time:** 1-2 hours
> **Why This Matters:** You can be the best coder in the world, but if you don't apply, you won't get hired.

### The Reality of Job Hunting

Most developers make this mistake: they wait until they're "ready" to start applying.

**The truth:**

- You're never 100% ready
- Interview feedback teaches you what to learn
- The hiring process takes 1-3 months
- Rejections early = lessons learned early
- Companies have seasonal hiring cycles

### Interview Preparation

**Interview Stages (Typical):**

```
1. RESUME SCREENING
   → ATS filters + recruiter review
   → Optimize resume with keywords

2. RECRUITER CALL (15-30 min)
   → Basic screening
   → Salary expectations
   → Availability

3. ONLINE ASSESSMENT (OA) (60-90 min)
   → 2-3 DSA problems
   → Sometimes MCQs
   → Timed

4. TECHNICAL INTERVIEW 1 (45-60 min)
   → DSA problem solving
   → Live coding (LeetCode style)
   → Explain your thinking

5. TECHNICAL INTERVIEW 2 (45-60 min)
   → System design (for experienced)
   → Project deep-dive
   → Language/framework specific

6. HIRING MANAGER (30-45 min)
   → Cultural fit
   → Behavioral questions
   → Career goals

7. HR ROUND (30 min)
   → Salary negotiation
   → Notice period
   → Offer details
```

**Prepare These Answers:**

**"Tell me about yourself" (2 minutes):**

```
"Hi, I'm [Name], a full-stack developer specializing in React and Node.js.

I recently graduated from [University] with a degree in [Field].
During my studies, I built several projects including [Project 1],
which [brief achievement].

I'm particularly passionate about [specific area] because [reason].

I'm looking for a role where I can contribute to [type of work]
while growing as an engineer.

I'm excited about [Company] because [specific reason]."
```

**"Why this company?":**

- Research the company beforehand
- Mention specific products, culture, values
- Connect to your own interests/goals

**"What's your greatest weakness?":**

- Be honest but strategic
- Show self-awareness
- Demonstrate improvement

```
"I sometimes spend too much time trying to perfect code before
getting feedback. I've been working on this by setting time limits
for initial implementations and seeking early code reviews."
```

**Behavioral Questions (STAR Method):**

```
SITUATION: Set the scene
TASK: What was your responsibility?
ACTION: What did you do?
RESULT: What was the outcome?

Example: "Tell me about a challenging project"

S: "During my final year, our team had to build a complete
   e-commerce platform in 3 weeks."

T: "I was responsible for the entire backend API and database design."

A: "I created a detailed plan, used Prisma for faster development,
   implemented CI/CD early, and held daily syncs with the frontend team."

R: "We delivered on time with all features working. The project
   received the highest grade, and I learned the importance of
   planning and communication."
```

**Common Behavioral Questions:**

- Tell me about a time you failed
- How do you handle disagreements with teammates?
- Describe a project you're proud of
- How do you learn new technologies?
- Tell me about a time you had to meet a tight deadline

**Questions to Ask the Interviewer:**

```
About the role:
- What does a typical day look like?
- What are the biggest challenges for this role?
- How is success measured?

About the team:
- How big is the team? What's the structure?
- What's the tech stack?
- How does the team collaborate?

About growth:
- What does career progression look like?
- How does the company support learning?
- Are there mentorship opportunities?

About culture:
- How would you describe the company culture?
- What do you enjoy most about working here?
- What's the work-life balance like?
```

**Research Salaries First:**

| --------------- | --------------------------------------- |

**Salary Ranges (India, 2024-26):**

| Level   | Service Companies | Startups  | Product Companies |
| ------- | ----------------- | --------- | ----------------- |
| Fresher | 3-6 LPA           | 4-10 LPA  | 8-20 LPA          |
| 1-2 YOE | 5-10 LPA          | 8-15 LPA  | 15-30 LPA         |
| 3-5 YOE | 10-18 LPA         | 15-30 LPA | 30-50 LPA         |

_LPA = Lakhs Per Annum_

**Negotiation Rules:**

1. **Never give a number first** — let them make an offer
2. **Always negotiate** — at least try
3. **Know your worth** — research beforehand
4. **Consider total compensation** — base + bonus + equity + benefits
5. **Get it in writing** — before accepting
6. **Be professional** — don't burn bridges

**When Asked About Salary Expectations:**

```
Approach 1: Deflect early in the process
"I'm focused on finding the right fit. I'm sure we can agree
on a fair number once we determine I'm the right candidate."

Approach 2: Give a range (after offer)
"Based on my research and the responsibilities of this role,
I was expecting something in the range of X to Y."

Approach 3: After receiving an offer
"Thank you for the offer. I'm excited about the role at [Company].
Based on my skills and the market rate, I was hoping for something
closer to [X]. Is there flexibility?"
```

**If Offer is Too Low:**

```
"I appreciate the offer and I'm very interested in the role.
However, based on my research and the value I can bring, I was
expecting something closer to [X]. Is there room to discuss this?"
```

### Handling Rejection

**Reality Check:**

- You WILL be rejected. A lot. This is normal.
- Even great candidates face 90%+ rejection rates for applications.
- Rejection is feedback, not failure.

**Expected Conversion Rates:**

```
Applications → Responses:     5-15%
Responses → Interviews:       30-50%
Interviews → Offers:          20-40%

Math: To get 1 offer, you might need:
100 applications → 10 responses → 4 interviews → 1 offer
```

**After Rejection:**

1. **Ask for feedback** (most won't respond, some will)
2. **Analyze** — Was it DSA? Projects? Communication?
3. **Improve** — Focus on the weak area
4. **Move on** — Apply to 5 more companies immediately
5. **Don't take it personally** — Fit matters, luck matters

**Rejection Response Template:**

```
Hi [Name],

Thank you for letting me know. While I'm disappointed, I
appreciate the opportunity to interview with [Company].

If possible, I'd greatly appreciate any feedback on areas
where I could improve.

I remain interested in [Company] and would love to be
considered for future opportunities.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

## Track B: Deep Fundamentals

> **Status:** 🔴 DEFERRED until employed
> **When to Start:** After 3 months settled in your first job
> **Daily Time:** Weekends only (3-5 hours)
> **Timeline:** 2-3 years for mastery

### Why Track B is Deferred

**Current Priority:** Get a job. Pay bills. Achieve financial stability.

**Track B is NOT cancelled.** It's postponed. Here's why:

1. **Financial reality** — You need income first
2. **Time constraints** — Can't do everything at once effectively
3. **Diminishing returns** — Deep CS knowledge doesn't help you get your FIRST job as much as Track A + DSA
4. **Long-term investment** — Track B pays off over years, not months

**After Employment:**

- You'll have income stability
- You can learn on weekends without pressure
- Work experience will make Track B concepts more meaningful
- You'll know which areas are most relevant to your career
