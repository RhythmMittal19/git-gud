# Buttons and Ports on a Computer

## 🔌 Connecting Peripherals

Ports are the connection points on the front and back of your computer case.

### Front of the Case

Usually contains easy-access ports:

- **Power Button:** Turns the computer on/off.
- **Optical Drive:** For CD/DVDs.
- **Audio Ports:** For headphones/microphones.
- **USB Ports:** For flash drives.

### Back of the Case

> _[Image: back of a computer case]_

- **Power Socket:** Where the power cord connects.
- **Ethernet Port:** Looks like a large phone jack; for networking/internet.
- **USB Ports:** For connecting mice, keyboards, printers.
- **Monitor Port:** Connects the screen (VGA, HDMI, DisplayPort).
- **Serial/Parallel Ports:** Older ports for legacy printers/peripherals.
- **Expansion Slots:** For adding new video or network cards.

### Peripherals (Plug-ins)

- **Printers:** Inkjet, Laser, Photo, All-in-one.
- **Scanners:** Copies physical images to digital ones.
- **Speakers/Headphones:** Output devices for sound.
- **Webcams:** Input devices for video recording/chatting.
